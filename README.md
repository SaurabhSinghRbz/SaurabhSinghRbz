## What I actually do

I'm the engineer teams hand the money-path to. My work sits where **reliability is a feature users pay for**: message queues that must process exactly once (a duplicate activates a gift card twice; a drop leaves a customer with dead plastic), notification pipelines that deliver every OTP while a user stares at a login screen, and card-lifecycle services where every state transition is auditable and reversible.

My habit, carried over from retraining out of mechanical engineering: **assume nothing is magic — read one layer deeper than the task requires.** It's why I migrate message brokers by benchmarking them first, and why my services ship with tracing before they ship with features.

## Engineering impact

- **Built and ran a B2B queue platform for 18 months as a team of one** — Node.js, MongoDB, Redis, RBAC, bulk uploads, per-message tracing with replay, webhooks and a Vue ops console. 500+ TPS sustained, millions of messages daily, exactly-once card activations for enterprise gift programs.
- **Cut a linear infrastructure cost to near-zero** by migrating the message broker from per-API-call pricing (Iron.io) to self-managed ActiveMQ — designed as a pluggable provider layer, so the cutover was config, not a rewrite.
- **Own the core of a Go gift-card lifecycle service** (chi, GORM, MySQL read/write split, Redis) behind HDFC, Axis, FairPrice and woohoo.in storefronts — activation, shipping, wallet credit, PIN reset, block/cancel/reissue. Its monitor-queue architecture activates bulk orders of **100,000 cards** through idempotent, retryable async stages.
- **Extended the notifications backbone** that delivers every email, SMS and WhatsApp message on the platform — OTP flows with brute-force lockout, versioned templates, a PII-encryption proxy in front of the GraphQL layer, and delivery-receipt tracking across a dozen vendor APIs.
- **Shipped a multi-tenant payments platform solo**: NestJS + Prisma API, Stripe with Apple Pay & Google Pay, multicurrency payment links, Socket.IO live order tracking, RBAC, merchant dashboard and customer QR-ordering app. Live in production for UAE merchants.

## Engineering philosophy

> **Own it like it's yours — because it is.**
> My first major service had a team of one. I learned to write the design doc, challenge it, build it, and wear the pager for it. I haven't shipped anything I wasn't willing to operate since.

> **Cost is a feature.**
> The cheapest way to say "senior" is a bill you made smaller. Broker migrations aren't glamorous — they're benchmarks and careful cutovers that pay for themselves many times over.

> **Reliability is designed, not debugged.**
> Exactly-once processing, idempotent retries, dead-letter queues and status contracts are decisions you make before the first message flows — not patches after the first incident.

> **AI is leverage, not a substitute for judgment.**
> I use AI agents daily for scaffolding, migrations and test coverage — and keep the architecture, the review and the accountability. The tools got faster; the standards didn't move.
